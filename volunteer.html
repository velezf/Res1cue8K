Volunteer-Thank you Volunteers!

Thank you to all our volunteers!

Thanks for volunteering for the inaugural Res1cue Run! Click the link below to visit out Sign Up Genius page to confirm your going to be able to help out.

The Rescue1Run needs about 100 volunteers in order to conduct race day activities. If you have a specific job in mind please look at the descriptions and see which fits you best.

Contact: martha.gaffney@bccrs.org

Res1cue Run Volunteer Job Descriptions

Bib Prep/ Packet Stuffing: 18 years and older. Volunteers will apply stickers and chips to race bibs. Packet stuffers will insert flyers into race bags.

Bib Pick Up: 18 years and older. Volunteers will verify runner identity and distribute race bibs/packets.

Start Area Set Up: 18 years and older. Volunteers will assist with assembling start line equipment, controlling pre-race crowds, and disassembly after the race.

Aid Station: 12 years and older. Set up and assist with distributing water/Gatorade throughout the race.

Course Marshal: 18 years and older. Course Marshals will be assigned along streets and intersections to prevent vehicle and spectator encroachment on the course.

Finish Line Setup: 18 years and older. Volunteers will assist with assembling finish line equipment, controlling post-race crowds, and disassembly after the race.

Finish Line Assistant: 12 years and older. Volunteers will assist with finish line water tables and chip collection.

Kids Ambulance Chase: 12 years and older. Volunteers will assist the Kids Ambulance Chase captain. Many fun different roles.

Clean Up: 18 years and older. Final clean up and assisting with return of equipment.

 

 

Many Volunteer Positions Still Available

Thank you for considering volunteering for the Res1cue Run. There are a variety of ways in which you can help. We have open positions for: course setup/break down, course marshals, aid stations, packet pickup and registration.
